    Early evolution of cartilaginous fishes

    PhD student, Imperial College London, Silwood Park Campus
    r.dearden14@ic.ac.uk
     @Euphanerops

    My PhD research focuses on the early evolution of the cartilaginous fishes - the group comprising living sharks, rays, and chimaeras. I primarily do this by studying fossils of some of their very earliest relatives, an extinct group known as "acanthodians" or spiny sharks. To visualise the anatomy of these animals I use methods such as micro-computed tomography (CT) scanning and x-ray synchrotron tomography. I then use this anatomical information to try and understand how these early cartilaginous fishes are related to one another and to their living relatives, and to piece together how the anatomy of these living cartilaginous fishes evolved.
