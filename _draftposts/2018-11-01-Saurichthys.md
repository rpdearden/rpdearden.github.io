As Triassic fishes go *Saurichthys* is pretty big dog(-fish?), even having its own non-stub Wikipedia article, but it struck me that I don't actually know very much about it. So I've made a fora done wrote a blog post.

In numerous pieces of aquatic Triassic palaeoart (admittedly, not a huge genre) you will notice a fish swimming, eating, or being eaten, in a rather pike-like manner. This fish is not a pike, but is rather *Saurichthys* - an early ray finned fish, and the hero of this tale.  The family it belongs to - the saurichthyiforms - all look like *Saurichthys*.  They are impressively widespread, with 40 species being found from the end of the Permian through to the Jurassic, from marine, freshwater, and brackish settings worldwide.
