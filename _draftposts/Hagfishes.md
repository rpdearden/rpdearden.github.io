*In which a slender-snouted saurichthyiform skeleton is scanned, studied, and surprises.*

-historical set up: paraphyletic vs monophyly
-key problem: long branches with taxa on ends, and nothing in between that preserved very good, characters difficult as no clear outgroup condition for things like the inner ear
-Adding to the confusion are various fossils which could be stem-gnaths or stem-cyclos
-Morphological evidence is such that are hagfish secondarily primitive?
-Higuchi et al. found evidence that the inner ear is secondarily derived in hagfishes
-Miyashita et al describe a hagfish from Cretaceous with many hagfishy traits, showing already present (Carb one?)
-They also run a (new?) morph analysis - come out monophyletic
-Another interesting thing is that some fossils come out as stem-cyclos, been knowcking around for a few years but little evidence.  Really needs more characters?
